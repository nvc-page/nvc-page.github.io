EARLY COLLECTIONS AND RESEARCH

Cacti against a wall at the east end of the Linnean House. Today, this area contains cacti discovered by George Engelmann.
Dr. George Engelmann, noted botanist and primary advisor to Henry Shaw, was a widely published expert on cacti. Engelmann described and named many species of cacti, and willed his plant collection to the Garden. In the Cohen Court Garden, you’ll find a display of cacti discovered and described by Engelmann.

THE LEGACY OF LAD CUTAK

Lad Cutak with the Garden’s cacti collection. Photo originally appeared in the May-June 1969 Bulletin, courtesy of the St. Louis Post-Dispatch
Ladislaus Cutak played a key role in expanding the Garden’s collection of cacti. Cutak joined the Garden in 1927 as a temporary laborer. His responsibilities grew over the years, as did his love of cactus. As superintendent of the greenhouses, he greatly expanded the Garden’s living collection. By 1941 it was the largest collection in the world of succulent plants under glass.

THE DESERT HOUSE

Cactus in shipping crate from Arizona. Greenhouse in background shows door sign ‘Entrance’ and ‘Desert Plants’.
The Garden’s Desert House opened to the public in 1913 and displayed multiple species of cacti. When the Desert House closed in 1994, the plant collection was moved to the Garden’s greenhouses. Select plants from the collection are now displayed in the Linnean House.
