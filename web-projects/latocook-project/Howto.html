How to make dashi

Dashi (だし) is the basic stock used for Japanese cooking. My best guess is if you are not familiar with Japanese ingredient, when you see “dashi” in my ingredient lists and you might have thought to yourself, what’s dashi… can I skip it?

Ingredients

For Dashi Packet:

2-3 cups water (420-720ml)

1 dashi packet (9g)

For Dashi Powder:

2 cups water (420ml)

1 tsp dashi powder (3g)

For Homemade Dashi:

⅓ oz kombu (dried kelp)(10g)

4 cups water (roughly 1000ml)

1-3 cups katsuobushi(10-30g)

Recipe Notes

Water: Use 2 cups of water for rich dashi and 3 cups for regular use.

How to make baked bhicken katsu

Juicy, tender, and crispy baked chicken katsu served with bright spring salad. In this recipe, chicken breast is coated in toasted panko and baked in the oven until golden brown. It makes a much healthier stand-in for greasy fried chicken. So simple and easy, you want to make this tonight

Ingredients

1 cup panko

1 Tbsp extra virgin olive oil

1 lb boneless skinless chicken breast

Freshly ground black pepper

¼ cup all-purpose flour

1 large egg

1 Tbsp water

Tonkatsu sauce

How to make vegetarian ramen

This Vegetarian Ramen recipe is going to rock your world! Learn the secret of creating a super rich & creamy broth without any meat bones.

Ingredients

For Vegetarian Dashi:

2-3 cups water (420-720ml)

1 piece kombu (dried kelp)

1 dried shiitake mushroom

For Soup:

2 cloves garlic

½ inch ginger

green onion/scallion (use white part)

1 Tbsp sake

Dash white pepper powder

For Ramen:

1 ramen noodles

Recipe Notes

Serving Size: If you want to make more than one serving, simply multiply the quantities of ingredients by the number of servings you wish to make.
